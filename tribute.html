Here's a time line of Mary Slessor's life

  • 1848- Born in Aberdeen,Scotland.
  • 1859- Family moved to Dundee in search of work. She began to work as a part timer in Baxter Brother's Mill spending less time in school and more in the factory.
  • 1875- Mary started her mission at the age of 27 and applied to the training of Foreign Mission Board of the United Presbyterian Church.
  • 1876- She set sail after her training in Edinburgh with her cousin Robert Mitchell Beedie who wasa missionary from New Deer in Buchan and arrived in West Africa over a month later. she was first assigned to the calabar region in the land of Efik people
  • 1879- She was forced to return to Scotlandto recover contracting malaria while trying to go deeper into calabar
  • 1880- She returned to calabar but 3 miles farther into calabar,in Old Town.
  • 1888- Mary travelled north to Okoyong and lived there for 15 years as well learnt the native language.
  • 1892- Slessor became vice-consul in Okoyong, presiding over the native court.
  • 1905- She was named vice president of Ikot Obong native court.
  • 1913- She was awarded the Order of St John. she began to suffer in her later years,but she remained in calabar
  • 1915- She died
